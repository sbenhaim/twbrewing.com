Short Circuit Stout

Short Circuit Stout is a full-bodied and roasty stout with a lingering sweet aftertaste. It uses 5 different malts to create a variety of flavors that change in the course of taking a sip. It falls into the13B Sweet Stout style category as identified by the Beer Judge Certification Program (BJCP).

How's it Made

What's With the Name?

By definition, a short circuit is an abnormal connection between two nodes of an electric circuit, often resulting in overheating, fire or even explosion. To the Tin Whiskers crew, this is a great way to describe the crossover of the distinct roasted flavors from the malts and the sweet aftertaste from the unfermentable lactose added during the boil. If you really want to blow your mind, try it served over vanilla ice cream for a beer float!

Trivia

The 1986 comedy science fiction film, Short Circuit, grossed $5,346,808 it's opening weekend, earning a total of $40,697,761 in the US Box Office.

BJCP: Sweet Stout, Style 13B

Short Circuit Stout Recipe

60% Efficiency, 15 galloons

Mash Ingredients

25# Pale Malt Maris Otter 2row 3srm
4# Chocolate Malt 350srm
4# Malted Oats 1srm
3# Crystal 120 120srm
2# Flaked Barley 1.7srm
1.15# Roasted Barley 300srm
0.20# Black Malt 500srm

Boil Ingredients

60min - 4oz Cluster 7%A
30min - 2.25# Lactose
1min - 2oz East Kent Goldings 5%A

OG 1.066
FG 1.024
Est. 5.3%ABV

Flip Switch IPA

Flip Switch IPA is a hoppy American pale ale with a slight citrus aroma. It features a variety of American hops, with just the right amount of bitterness to match the malty base. It falls into the14B American IPA style category as identified by the Beer Judge Certification Program (BJCP)

How's it Made

What's With the Name?

A flip switch is a mechanism actuating the transition between open and closed states of an electrical circuit. Probably the most common example is a light switch. You know that intense bright light you get when you flip on a good old fashioned light bulb? The kind that almost blinds you until your eyes have a second to adjust? Well the bold hop flavor you experience in the first sip of a Flip Switch is kind of like that, without the blindness of course.

Trivia

In countries prone to earthquakes, such as Japan, most light switches are positioned sideways to prevent the switch from inadvertently being turned on or off by falling objects

BJCP: American IPA, Style 14B

Flip Switch IPA Recipe

60% Efficiency, 15 gallons

Mash Ingredients

30# Pale Malt 2row 2srm
10# Caramel/Crystal 20 20srm
3# Dextrine Malt 2srm

Boil Ingredients

60min - 3oz Chinook 11.4%A
20min - 2oz Warrior 16.8%A
10min - 1.5oz Centennial 10%A
1min - 2oz Citra 11%A

Dry Hop

4.5oz Citra 11%A
3oz Warrior 16.8%A

OG 1.066
FG 1.020
Est. 6.1%ABV

Wheatstone Bridge

Wheatstone Bridge is a light and refreshing wheat beer with distinct flavors of honey and chamomile tea. Made by adding Minnesota honey to an American malted wheat base, the final step involves steeping chamomile tea. It falls into the 6D American Wheat style category as identified by the Beer Judge Certification Program (BJCP).

How's it Made

What's With the Name?

Invented by Samuel Hunter Christie in 1833 and improved and popularized by Sir Charles Wheatstone in 1843, a Wheatstone bridge is an electrical circuit used to measure an unknown electrical resistance by balancing two legs of a bridge circuit, one leg of which includes the unknown component. But don't over think this one, instead picture Sir Charles Wheatstone basking in the sun, drinking a beer and enjoying the fame and popularity that undoubtedly comes with popularizing someone else's invention.

Trivia

While performing experiments in electricity as a teenager, Wheatsone ran short of the money he needed to buy copper-plates for building a battery. While looking at the few copper pennies he had left, a thought occurred, 'We must use the pennies themselves,' he said, and the battery was soon complete.

BJCP: American Style Wheat or Rye, Style 6D

Wheatstone Bridge Recipe

70% Efficiency, 15 gallons

Mash Ingredients

5# 14.3% Rice Hulls
12# 34.3% Pale Malt 2row 6srm
12# 34.3% Wheat Malt 2srm

Boil Ingredients

60min - 2oz Vanguard 5.5%A 12.9IBU
0min - 6# 17.1% Honey, 6oz Chamomile

OG 1.054
FG 1.005
Est 6.3%ABV

Schottky Pumpkin Ale

Schottky Pumpkin Ale is a with a sweet pumpkin flavor. Made with actual roasted pumpkin and generous amounts of pumpkin pie spice, it is a great beer to enhance any fall meal. It falls into the 21A Spice, Herb, or Vegetable Beer category as identified by the Beer Judge Certification Program (BJCP).

How's it Made

What's With the Name?

This beer is an homage to German physicist Walter H. Schottky (1888-1976) who played a major role in developing the theory of electron and ion emission phenomena. The shape you see carved into the pumpkin on our beer labels is actually the sign for the Schottky diode, which is a semiconductor diode with a low forward voltage drop and a very fast switching action.

Trivia

Schottky died on March 4, 1976 at the age of 90. His death came just two years after his former employer, Siemens, had begun commercially manufacturing Schottky diodes for microwave use.

BJCP: Spice, Herb, or Vegetable Beer, Style 21

Schottky Pumpkin Recipe

60% Efficiency, 15 gallons

Mash Ingredients

30# Pale Malt Maris Otther 2row 3srm
5# Pumpkin, canned, roasted
3# Victory Malt 25srm
2# Munich Malt 20srm
1# Crystal 60 60srm
0.5# Crystal 80 80srm

Mash at 160 for 60 minutes

Boil Ingredients

60min - 3oz Cluster 7aa
0min - 1oz Pumpkin pie spices

OG 13.6p
FG 3.5p

Ampere Biere

Ampere Biere is a light-bodied Bière de Garde style French ale with just the right amount of malty sweetness. It's a smooth and refreshing beer to drink year round. It falls into the 16D Bière de Garde category as identified by the Beer Judge Certification Program (BJCP).

How's it Made

What's With the Name?

An ampere (or amp) is a unit of electric current named after French mathematician and physicist André-Marie Ampère (1775–1836). In addition to being the father of electrodynamics, Ampère was an all around solid guy, described as having "great amiability and childlike simplicity of character." You could use these same exact words to describe our solid, year-round Ampere Biere.

Trivia

Andre Marie Ampere was dogged by bad luck. His father, for example, was guillotined during the French Revolution and his wife died shortly after their marriage. On his deathbed in 1836, he ordered that an inscription be placed upon his tombstone: Tandem Felix (Happy at Last).

BJCP: Biere de Garde, Style 16D

Ampere Biere Recipe

60% Efficiency, 15 gallons

Mash Ingredients

38# Pilsner 2row
6# Vienna Malt 3.5srm
2# Aromatic Malt 26srm
1.5# Honey Malt 25srm

Mash 151F for 75 minutes

Boil Ingredients

60min - 4oz Styrian Goldings 5.4aa

OG 1.069
FG 1.020

One "Devoted connection" to beer.

The three founders of Tin Whiskers Brewing Co., LLC are George Kellerman, Jake Johnson, and Jeff Moriarty. They first met in 2005 while going to the University of Minnesota for bachelor degrees in Electrical Engineering. Over time, they all worked together as hardware design engineers for a small engineering services company specializing in RF-centric products. Being young engineers working downtown Minneapolis, they attended various happy hours together. It quickly became clear that they all really loved craft beer and were very intrigued with the science of the brewing process.

Jake and Jeff are the two brewers of the company who designed and honed the recipes. They got their start in 2006 brewing extract kits on Jake's Mom's kitchen stove much to her chagrin. (A few boil-overs did occur on her stove, whoops!) They brewed countless extract kits until in 2010, when after touring Surly and Flat Earth, they were inspired to turn their passion (like the founders of those breweries) into a business. From that point forward it has been all grain brewing and loving it. Nowadays, Jake focuses on the brewing aspect of the business while Jeff focuses on the brewing as well as the business side of the operation.

George, in the early years, was a force within the brewery by helping out at every brew session and providing needed muscle for cleaning and moving material around the brewhouse. He focused a lot on the quality of our beer and pushed us to design better more complex beers. He has moved on to be in charge of our online presence including developing our own beer wiki engine.

What's a Tin Whisker

A Tin Whisker is is an electrical engineering term for a type of product fault that typically has the symptom of a short circuit on the printed circuit board (PCB). It is caused by a tin hair that would grow between adjacent pins of an integrated circuit (IC which are the little black parts you'll see on the circuit board). Read more on Wikipedia
