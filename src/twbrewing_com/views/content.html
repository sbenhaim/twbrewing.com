Short Circuit Stout

Short Circuit Stout is a full-bodied and roasty stout with a lingering sweet aftertaste. It uses 5 different malts to create a variety of flavors that change in the course of taking a sip. It falls into the 13B Sweet Stout style category as identified by the Beer Judge Certification Program (BJCP).

How's It Made?

By definition, a short circuit is an abnormal connection between two nodes of an electric circuit, often resulting in overheating, fire or even explosion. To the Tin Whiskers crew, this is a great way to describe the crossover of the distinct roasted flavors from the malts and the sweet aftertaste from the unfermentable lactose added during the boil. If you really want to blow your mind, try it served over vanilla ice cream for a beer float!

BJCP: Sweet Stout, Style 13B

Short Circuit Stout Recipe

60% Efficiency, 15 galloons

Mash Ingredients

25# Pale Malt Maris Otter 2row 3srm
4# Chocolate Malt 350srm
4# Malted Oats 1srm
3# Crystal 120 120srm
2# Flaked Barley 1.7srm
1.15# Roasted Barley 300srm
0.20# Black Malt 500srm

Boil Ingredients

60min - 4oz Cluster 7%A
30min - 2.25# Lactose
1min - 2oz East Kent Goldings 5%A

OG 1.066
FG 1.024
Est. 5.3%ABV

Flip Switch IPA

Flip Switch IPA is an award winning American Style IPA, BJCP style 14B. This style is known for its intense hop aroma and flavor. Maris Otter was chosen as a base malt for the biscuity flavor it imparts along with other specialty grains to add color and body. American hops (i.e., Chinook, Warrior, Centennial, and Citra) impart bold aromas, flavors, and a balanced bitterness to match the malty base.

BJCP: American IPA, Style 14B

Flip Switch IPA Recipe

60% Efficiency, 15 gallons

Mash Ingredients

30# Pale Malt 2row 2srm
10# Caramel/Crystal 20 20srm
3# Dextrine Malt 2srm

Boil Ingredients

60min - 3oz Chinook 11.4%A
20min - 2oz Warrior 16.8%A
10min - 1.5oz Centennial 10%A
1min - 2oz Citra 11%A

Dry Hop

4.5oz Citra 11%A
3oz Warrior 16.8%A

OG 1.066
FG 1.020
Est. 6.1%ABV

Wheatstone Bridge

We used American malted wheat as the base beer and added Minnesota honey to give a nice light and silky base into which chamomile tea is then steeped. All this combines to create a light refreshing beer that is perfect for relaxing while enjoying the summer weather.

BJCP: American Style Wheat or Rye, Style 6D

Wheatstone Bridge Recipe

70% Efficiency, 15 gallons

Mash Ingredients

5# 14.3% Rice Hulls
12# 34.3% Pale Malt 2row 6srm
12# 34.3% Wheat Malt 2srm

Boil Ingredients

60min - 2oz Vanguard 5.5%A 12.9IBU
0min - 6# 17.1% Honey, 6oz Chamomile

OG 1.054
FG 1.005
Est 6.3%ABV

Schottky Pumpkin Recipe

Who wouldn't want pumpkin pie in the form of a beer? This beer is the perfect complement to fall. With its delicious roasted pumpkin in the mash combined with lots of pumpkin pie spice, it makes for a great beer that enhances any fall meal.

BJCP: Spice, Herb, or Vegetable Beer, Style 21

Schottky Pumpkin Recipe

60% Efficiency, 15 gallons

Mash Ingredients

30# Pale Malt Maris Otther 2row 3srm
5# Pumpkin, canned, roasted
3# Victory Malt 25srm
2# Munich Malt 20srm
1# Crystal 60 60srm
0.5# Crystal 80 80srm

Mash at 160 for 60 minutes

Boil Ingredients

60min - 3oz Cluster 7aa
0min - 1oz Pumpkin pie spices

OG 13.6p
FG 3.5p

Ampere Biere

This is a nice light bodied beer with the just-right amount of malt flavor that makes it perfect to drink year round. Our change from typical biere de gardes was to use a lager yeast that we ferment at lower ale temps. This simple beer leaves you feeling refreshed.

BJCP: Biere de Garde, Style 16D

Ampere Biere Recipe

60% Efficiency, 15 gallons

Mash Ingredients

38# Pilsner 2row
6# Vienna Malt 3.5srm
2# Aromatic Malt 26srm
1.5# Honey Malt 25srm

Mash 151F for 75 minutes

Boil Ingredients

60min - 4oz Styrian Goldings 5.4aa

OG 1.069
FG 1.020

One "Devoted connection" to beer.

The three founders of Tin Whiskers Brewing Co., LLC are George Kellerman, Jake Johnson, and Jeff Moriarty. They first met in 2005 while going to the University of Minnesota for bachelor degrees in Electrical Engineering. Over time, they all worked together as hardware design engineers for a small engineering services company specializing in RF-centric products. Being young engineers working downtown Minneapolis, they attended various happy hours together. It quickly became clear that they all really loved craft beer and were very intrigued with the science of the brewing process.

Jake and Jeff are the two brewers of the company who designed and honed the recipes. They got their start in 2006 brewing extract kits on Jake's Mom's kitchen stove much to her chagrin. (A few boil-overs did occur on her stove, whoops!) They brewed countless extract kits until in 2010, when after touring Surly and Flat Earth, they were inspired to turn their passion (like the founders of those breweries) into a business. From that point forward it has been all grain brewing and loving it. Nowadays, Jake focuses on the brewing aspect of the business while Jeff focuses on the brewing as well as the business side of the operation.

George, in the early years, was a force within the brewery by helping out at every brew session and providing needed muscle for cleaning and moving material around the brewhouse. He focused a lot on the quality of our beer and pushed us to design better more complex beers. He has moved on to be in charge of our online presence including developing our own beer wiki engine.

What's a Tin Whisker

A Tin Whisker is is an electrical engineering term for a type of product fault that typically has the symptom of a short circuit on the printed circuit board (PCB). It is caused by a tin hair that would grow between adjacent pins of an integrated circuit (IC which are the little black parts you'll see on the circuit board). Read more on Wikipedia
