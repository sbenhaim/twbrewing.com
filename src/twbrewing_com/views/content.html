Short Circuit Stout

Short Circuit Stout is a full-bodied and roasty stout with a lingering sweet aftertaste. It uses 5 different malts to create a variety of flavors that change in the course of taking a sip. It falls into the 13B Sweet Stout style category as identified by the Beer Judge Certification Program (BJCP).

How's It Made?

By definition, a short circuit is an abnormal connection between two nodes of an electric circuit, often resulting in overheating, fire or even explosion. To the Tin Whiskers crew, this is a great way to describe the crossover of the distinct roasted flavors from the malts and the sweet aftertaste from the unfermentable lactose added during the boil. If you really want to blow your mind, try it served over vanilla ice cream for a beer float!

BJCP: Sweet Stout, style 13B

Short Circuit Stout Recipe

60% Efficiency, 15 galloons

Mash Ingredients

25# Pale Malt Maris Otter 2row 3srm
4# Chocolate Malt 350srm
4# Malted Oats 1srm
3# Crystal 120 120srm
2# Flaked Barley 1.7srm
1.15# Roasted Barley 300srm
0.20# Black Malt 500srm

Boil Ingredients

60min - 4oz Cluster 7%A 30min - 2.25# Lactose 1min - 2oz East Kent Goldings 5%A

One "Devoted connection" to beer.

The three founders of Tin Whiskers Brewing Co., LLC are George Kellerman, Jake Johnson, and Jeff Moriarty. They first met in 2005 while going to the University of Minnesota for bachelor degrees in Electrical Engineering. Over time they managed to all work together as hardware design engineers for a small engineering services company that specialized in doing RF centric products. Being young engineers working downtown Minneapolis they attended various happy hours and it quickly became clear that they each really loved craft beer and were very intrigued with the science of the brewing process.

Jake and Jeff are the two brewers of the company and designed and honed the recipes. They got their start brewing in 2006 doing extract kits on Jake's Mom's kitchen stove much to her chagrin (few boil overs did occurred on her stove. Whoops!) They brewed countless extract kits for many years until in 2010, when after touring Surly and Flat Earth they were inspired to turn their passion, like the founders of those breweries, into a business. From that point forward its been all grain brewing and loving it. Nowadays Jake focuses on the brewing aspect of the business. Jeff focuses on the brewing and on the business side of the operation.

George in the early years was a force within the brewery helping out at every brew session providing a lot of muscle with the cleaning and moving material around the brewhouse. He focused a lot on the quality of our beer and pushed us to design better more complex beers. He has moved on to be in charge of our online presence with the development of our own beer wiki engine.

We’re working on it.

The Tin Whiskers founders are busy getting the brewery up and running. If you’re interested in helping out or being a part of it, please contact Tin Whiskers. In the mean time, you'll only find Tin Whiskers beer in the basement brewery at founder, Jeff Moriarty's house.

Eventually this page will have all the tools necessary to track down the nearest Tin Whisker's pint, growlers, or 6-pack.
